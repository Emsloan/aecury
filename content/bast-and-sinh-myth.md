## Historicalbasis

Bast, progenitor goddess of the Crocodilians, became enamored with a Leonin named Sinh. Sinh was Champion of Anhur. During the Sowing, instead of Championing a crocodilian, Bast mated with Sinh. Unexpectedly, Sinh was invested with a fragment of her power. Sinh, possessing divine power from two gods, had legendary strength. Sinh's prowess enchanted many Leonin and in short time he gained a fervent following.

When news of Bast's transgression reached Anhur, who at the time was serving as Celestial Guardian, he took it as a great insult. In a fit of rage, Anhur struck the Celestial Gate, unwittingly weakening it temporarily. Demanding justice, Anhur pleaded with his brethren deities to punish Bast. Hathor eventually relented, and stripped Bast of her divinity. Bast's descent to mortality caused the crocodilians to succumb to a terrible affliction, causing them to lose their intelligence and be overwhelmed by a maddening rage. The Wahashan's were forced to drive them out of the city into the Jaws of Sebek.

Queen Alia Shevade the Unbroken, faced with a growing faction of Sinh's followers and the chaos of the Crocodilian Reduction, exiled Sinh. Sinh and Bast traveled north with a small number of their closest followers. This group's descendants would one day become the Tabaxi tribes of Zumarud.

Many years after their exile, a calamity threatened Zumarud. Sinh made the ultimate sacrifice to protect his home from disaster. Moments before death, Sinh returns Bast's fragment of divinity, allowing her to ascend once more as Sharess. Sinh's body perished but his soul was forever warped by the strain of his final fight, transforming into Malar.
