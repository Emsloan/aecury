## Spirituality

**Dawrat Allahab** (cycle of flame); **Alnaar al'abadia** (the eternal fire); **Shuelat alhayaa** (flame of life)

### Tayr A'byad, The Life-giving Flame

Also known as the First Phoenix, Tayr A'byad is the elder spirit believed to have given life to the Crimson Province and its denizens. Tayr A'byad is described as a massive, multi-winged white phoenix with rainbow tipped feathers. The Life-giving Flame is said to swirl around her head, forming a halo of fire.

_Eye Color: White or Multi-colored Iris_

### Dhahab, The Cunning One

The Cunning One is the elder spirit associated with greed and malice. Stories told by connected of the First Phoenix describe the Cunning One's attempts at taking Tayr A'byad's Life-giving Flame for their own. Dhahab is described as an enormous golden fox with six legs, each adorned in bracelets and jewels. A single, bright green gem is embedded in his forehead. His tail is missing after a legendary battle with Ardra, the Stalwart.

_Eye Color: Golden-yellow Iris_

### Ardra, The Stalwart

Ardra is a mountain-sized black lizard that is said to live deep within the earth. With wings of obsidian and flowing gold in its veins, connected of Ardra believe it is she who holds up the ground and allows them to live in the light of The Flame.

_Eye Color: Gray-black Iris_

### Ithish, The Jovial Serpent

The Jovial Serpent is believed to exist in the skies above the Crimson Province, bringing with them joy and luck to those blessed beneath them.

_Eye Color: Green Iris_

## Areas of the Crimson Province

1. **Mimic Zone**
   - This area is infested with mimics. Mimics replace most objects, buildings, and sometimes… people.

2. **Crystal Plague Zone**
   - People are cursed with a crystal plague. Most die within weeks as their body is overtaken by crystal. Lungs or heart solidifies, flesh becomes immobile. A small percentage survive, the encroachment stops. However, it is merely dormant. Seemingly random, months or years in the future, the crystal plague reawakens and transforms them into a mindless crystal monster.

3. **Random Magical Intelligence Zone**
   - The region is afflicted with a perpetual effect, where any living creatures in the area might gain vastly increased intelligence. These affects could last for minutes, days, or even years and then with no warning go away. This leads to strange 'start-up' civilizations of animals. A vast squirrel empire might spring up nearly overnight, and then disappear without a trace years later, leaving their cities mysteriously abandoned.

4. **Gravitational Disturbance Zone**
   - In this region the force of gravity has been altered. Some areas have crushingly strong gravity, making travel there painfully slow. Others have lighter gravity, allowing jumps of hundreds of feet. Some regions have shifting gravity, with floating boulders that might fall without warning.

5. **Fernaxum Zone**
   - This region has lots of purple flames, fire elementals, lights, and other stuff.

6. **Haunt Zone**
   - Frantic ghosts. The souls of those surrounding the capital are forever changed to Aecury.

7. **Symbolic Insanity 'Uzumaki' Zone**
   - This region has been twisted. At first glance it seems normal. However, the longer one spends there the more the truth is revealed. One begins to see strange fractal patterns. Some report spirals, others geometric crosses that repeat ad infinitum. Viewing these patterns causes one to slowly go insane or achieve enlightenment, depending on one's perspective.

8. **Moonlike Zone**
   - This region, the epicenter of the impact, is dominated by a massive crater. It resembles in many ways the surface of Iadath. The sky above this area is perpetually dark, suffused with unfamiliar stars and cosmos. The center of this crater is a lone citadel, made of a mysterious stone. Its architecture is strange and seemingly doesn't follow the laws of engineering or even rational geometry. The citadel was the remnant of the original elven civilization. It serves as the only known entrance to the underground, and by extension the Golden City. It also hosts a direct portal to Iadath.

## The Lands of the Halim

Post flood, Halim was populated by scattered villages and small fiefdoms. The people called themselves Al Halim. They worshiped the Elder Aspects, ancient spirits who personified a creature and an aspect of personality.

When dreaming, the Al Halim people were connected by a shared dream realm. Therein they could interact and communicate with each other and ancestral spirits.

## Basim Bhatia

Basim, a disguised Arcanoloth, was reincarnated according to Asmodeus' will as a child of Aecury, part of the Al Halim people of the Crimson Province. Raised as an unassuming mortal commoner, upon her 16th year Basim awoke to her terrible purpose. After a decade of genius political maneuvering Basim was crowned Princeps of the Crimson Province.

It was then that Basim started the difficult work of corrupting the Crimson Province, with the goal of forcing them to rely on fiendish powers. Basim sequestered herself for one years time, much to the terror of her supplicants, during much of which she astral projected. Eventually she found the Hidden Library of Abmyz, trading the secret of her true nature in exchange for access to the knowledge within. In those moldering stacks she hatched her master plan.

Returning to her physical body, she put out a call for adventurers from across the Province. Many came and most died, save for a group that came to be known as the Champions of Basim. She tasked them with finding the Temple of Nerglesh and slaying the slumbering monstrosity within, bringing its heart back as proof. As a reward, she swore they would find themselves at the root of an eternal empire, their names never forgotten.

The creature within the temple was indeed horrific, a beholder of immense size and age, put under a perpetual sleeping spell by Sahtekar the Binder in ages past. The Temple of Nerglesh was warped by the beholder's presence and infested with vermin spawned from its dreams, but the Champions of Basim prevailed.

The Champions presented Princeps Basim with the heart and Basim kept her promise. She ordered her royal guard to slaughter the Champions and bury their corpses deep in the dungeons of her castle and canonized them as martyred saints, who perished in her service.

The heart was actually the beholder's womb, the eggs within kept in stasis for eons as their mother slept. Basim, using the knowledge gained from Abmyz, had her court sages perform a dark spell on the unborn beholder's. The unborn were sacrificed, creating a nightmare curse that swept over the Province. Their ancestral dream spirits were corrupted and warped, plaguing the Al Halim people with visions of inescapable horror each time they slept.

Pleased with her work, Basim waited as the curse slowly drove her people closer to the brink of madness, leaving the Province in search of one who could help her with the next phase of her scheme. Years into her travels, she found her answer in the depths of an ancient pyramid, a relic of the long dead Esprin Empire on Kravral. Hidden murals depicting profaned rituals told her of a people who severed the wings of their very souls. Their essence trapped within their bodies forever, the people achieved a form of twisted immortality.

Searching for the secrets behind the ritual she encountered a familiar name: Sahtekar the Binder. Thinking the ancient magus dead she implored Asmodeus for the first time, certain the soul of one responsible for such horror would reside in hell. To her surprise, she learned Sahtekar was not dead, instead living as a hermit on the summit of the Fingers.

Basim visited Sahtekar and immediately Sahtekar saw through her guise. Impressed, Basim dropped all pretense of a regent in need. Instead, she told him of the suffering of her people, proudly claiming responsibility for their plight. It was evident to both that the Crimson Province would soon perish under the weight of madness.

As the ancient magus considered this, Basim struggled to stifle a predatory grin as she saw the regret and pain in Sahtekar's eyes. Basim told Sahtekar of the solution, one he himself had already surmised but dared not enact: sever her people's connection to the Realm of Dreams, like he had done to his own people so long ago. Sahtekar refused and Basim returned to the Crimson Province.

For the next 30 nights Sahtekar had the same dream, a gift from Basim's own court sages. The dream contained no fanciful imagery or merciful deceptions, merely faithful recreations of the horrors of the Crimson Province. After the 30th night, Sahtekar could not allow the suffering to continue. Sahtekar came to Basim, and together they developed a spell according to Basim's goals. When the development was done Sahtekar invoked the Will and cast the spell, and thus the Dreamless were born.

For many years the Province rebuilt. Led by Princeps Basim, now worshiped by many as a God Empress for saving them from the nightmare, the Dreamless found themselves changed. They were unable to imagine, create, or invent. It seemed the act of dreaming invigorated the minds of mortals and the Dreamless were hopelessly mundane as a result. God Empress Basim, infinitely caring, sought a solution to her people's plight.

Under the pretense of entreating the gods for aid, Basim underwent intense negotiations with her fellow devil-kin. She offered myriad mortal hosts, each ripe for corruption. In return all she asked for was a portion of the glory when the devils inevitably gave their mortal souls to Asmodeus. The accord was struck and Basim came to her people with a miracle. Basim taught them a simple ritual, performed on the night of conception and the day of birth, wherein the parents would invoke a hidden name of a great dream spirit. Doing this would ensure a child was born with a guardian ancestor spirit, upon which they could draw from their vast experiences as a replacement for their so sweet dreams.

Privately, those who had direct contact with the Princeps began to refer to her in secret as the Grinning Lady, for it seemed she had a perpetual smile on her face. In truth, Basim simply couldn't contain her satisfaction.

## Peoples of the Crimson Province

### The Corrupted

Basim aligned, devil folk.

### The Anathema

The nightmare afflicted, who never had their connection severed, and learned to live with nightmare spirits.

**Appearance:**

- Hollow eyes, sunken in, dark circles
- Pale, nearly translucent skin; veins can be easily seen within
- Black hair
- Vibrant, intense colored eyes (red, gold, violet)
- Black and gray clothing with silver metal bracelets
- Glass beads and plates often worked into their clothing

**Personality:** Angry, ill-tempered. Curt. Short of speech. Incredibly pragmatic. Might is right.

**Abilities:**

- Psychic Tempering, resistance to psychic damage
- Limited clairvoyance; the mad ancestor spirits gained a measure of knowledge when they were twisted. Sometimes the knowledge is of things yet to pass
- Good initiative rolls; choose to get advantage on saving throws
- Limited truesight

**Equipment:** They wear intricate visors or masks to spare them the brunt of the visions caused by their mad ancestor spirits. The creation of these masks is a closely guarded secret. They function like dream catchers, but for vision.

**Behavior:** They are some of the few who range into the deep zones near the center of impact. The ghosts and maddening symbols are nothing compared to the visions their spirits grant them.

### The Dreamless

Those who had their connection severed, but never accepted devils.

**Appearance:**

- Dark skinned, similar to pure Al Halim, except for eyes and hair
- Monochromatic pupils (grays, silver, black, and rarely tan/sand colored)
- Hair is dull gray, even at a young age
- Simple clothing completely lacking in aesthetics and adornment
- Unfinished edging on fabrics; rope closures instead of buttons or belts
- Clothing is loose and ill-fitted, one-size fits all
- Neutral toned and undyed, often patched by whatever textiles are available
- Long, wild hair; not dirty, but not particularly clean. Like animals

**Tattoos:** The only exception is a seeming fascination with geometric tattoos. Triangles, arrows, circles, and rectangles across their bodies, always symmetrical. All ages are tattooed, and the tattooing is seemingly compulsive, as people tattoo themselves. In the absence of needles and ink, they use scarification as a substitute. Some believe this obsession is a sign that the Dreamless have come to understand some greater order of the world.

**Abilities:**

- Simplistic, unimaginative speech
- Struggle to convey complicated or abstract ideas
- Often underestimate intelligence
- Very perceptive
- Very patient
- Does not need to sleep, though still needs to rest their body
- Advantage on intelligence saving throws

### The Exorcized

Those who conquered their devils and came out on top in charge of their destiny.

### The Pure

Those who managed to resist the nightmares and avoid being cut off from the dream.

**Appearance:**

- Dark skinned, bedouin style clothing
- Primarily white clothing with bright colored accents dependent on the Elder Aspect they worship or are simply trying to exemplify
- Black hair
- Light pastel colored eyes (blues, pinks, greens, yellows, oranges, and purple)

**Behavior:** They ride guard drakes, and herd Aurochs and camels.
