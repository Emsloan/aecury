In the southern expanse of [[Zumarud]] a vast desert stretches from coast to coast. Peeking out from the rolling dunes are the hints of ancient structures, left by the now destroyed Jinshu Imperium.

During the height of their reign, the Jinshu Imperium had absolute rule of nearly half of Zumarud, at one time even threatening the supremacy of [[Wahasha, the Beasthold]] and [[Oroboros]] to the north.

Archaeological records show that the Jinshu had at least 5 sprawling settlements, referred to even in their time as the Shaded Kingdoms, though details pertaining to two are scarce: Each city is built in a different geographic feature. (Crater, Canyon, etc.)
[ul]
[li]City of Glass - Glasswork everywhere. Stained glass, scientific flasks on a grand scale, glass dyed dark to block out the twinsun, glass with radioactive additives to make it glow in a unnatural green/pink/purple way.[/li]
[li]City of Gold - Heavy, geometric buildings. Brutalist architecture with highly detailed engraving and filigree on every surface. Gold is soft and beautiful. The city of artists and creatives.[/li]
[li]City of Bone - Center of rule, where the emperor lives. In a giant crater. The city that lives directly under the second sun, black iron bleached white by the light. Massive spires, grown up in odd seemingly random patterns. When a structure collapses, it was not demolished but included in the new structure in a nearly organic way. Progress at all costs. Reaching towards the second sun and eventually encapsulating it in the high palace. Spire a dark mirror of the Spire of Oronin.[/li]
[li]City of Blood - A city beneath the sands, even during it's heyday. Confusing mazelike tunnels, intersecting. Tenements, taverns, and temples built directly into the tunnel walls. Instead of trying to resist the sun, they sought shelter below where it is said that they found the secret truth within all things. Plague god.[/li]
[/ul]
