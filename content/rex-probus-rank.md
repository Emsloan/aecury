  [center][h2]The Gauntlet of the First Knight[/h2]
  [/center]
  The throne of Historia has been dedicated to the individual who most effectively represents the honor and duty shown in Helm’s. Because of this the throne is not inherited, it is earned. There have been 2 examples of inherited rulership; the second reigning monarch William the Stalwart, who succeeded his mother, was the first instance of an inherited throne. The second example is his daughter, Jessica the Fair, who succeeded her father. When it came time for Jessica to find a successor, she turned to Helm for guidance and found that the will of the First Knight was to look for his Paragon in the people, not the keep. This was the first step in the installation of the Gauntlet of the First Knight.
  
  The Gauntlet is a tournament dedicated to allowing the next ruler of Historia to rise to the occasion of showing their connection to Helm and display their abilities to lead. Anyone who feels they personify the ideals of Helm may enter the tournament, and participate in the first round of competition. The 16 contenders who persist past this first round become those who will compete for the throne. A council of the current Rex Probus, the Pontiff Ascendi, the Iudex Primeri, the Vox Platini Dei, and the 7 guild leaders of Historia rank the 16 competitors against each other and assign their place in the bracket. From there the duels take place over the course of the next month, whoever is left standing at the end of this tournament goes on to the true Gauntlet of the First Knight.
  
  The Gauntlet of the First Knight is held in the courtyard of Castle Historia, during the course of the week-long Gauntlet the Cleansing Vessel is lit and tended to by members of the Church of the Golden Sun. A small arena is built in the shape of a horseshoe with the Vessel positioned at the opening. Stands make up the loop, and a grandstand is placed opposite of the Vessel that holds seating for the Pillars, the Vox Platini Dei, the Guild Leaders, and the 6 highest ranking members of the Historian military. The seventh, from the top, ranking member of the military serves as the first test for the throne-seeker. Over the next week the challenger will work their way through a series of duels, one per day, against the members of the military. If the challenger successfully bests those officers they will have one week to rest and prepare themself for the final test, a duel with the current Paragon of Helm, the reigning Monarch of Historia.
