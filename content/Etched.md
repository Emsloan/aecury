The nomadic rangers of the [[Sandswimmers]]. Originally the Sandswimmers were a nomadic people, who made pilgrimages to the site where they first arrived in their new lands, [[Mawa]]. Eventually people settled there, until most of the populace no longer roamed the tunnels. Still, the Etched persisted as it was their charge to keep the people of Mawa safe. Over the years Mawa grew, as did the range of the Etched's forays until it was sometimes years before they would return.

The Etched's return was originally regarded as a time of celebration for it meant their protectors were safe and could take well deserved rests. However, the duties of the Etched exacted a heavy toll and they needed to replenish their ranks. So, during their times in Mawa, they would choose children who would leave their homes forever and learn to stalk the gloomy halls. As decades passed this ceremony became a thing the Sandswimmers feared. Grizzled and haunted men, who were now almost strangers, parleyed with monsters and committed atrocities, would take your children from you without a word.

They ride Chuuls.
