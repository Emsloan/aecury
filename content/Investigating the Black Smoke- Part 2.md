A plume of black smoke can sometimes be seen far in the distance, south of [[Raft]].

[[Otiz]], [[Morpho Shimmerwing]], [[Vulpes]], and [[Sussarra Kurva]] travel again to the outpost to investiage.

[b]The outpost is 30 miles southwest of Raft, as the crow flies. Each hex is 10 miles.[/b]

The weather of the first day of travel is chilly, cold for spring, approximately 50 degrees. There is no wind and the sky is clear. A DC 16 Nature check indicates that rain is likely in the next day, as the sudden cold can sometimes bring weather events. Sleeping in the cold without a fire is difficult. Characters who sleep without a source of warmth must make a DC 15 Con Save or wake with one point of exhaustion.

Noon of the first day players encounter statue:

[quote]

[i]It appears to depict a heavily robed female figure in intricately worked marble. The statue is broken around the waist and the top half lays to the right side.

Where the bottom of the robe would meet the ground, large octopus-like tentacles spill out in all directions in place of feet. The top half's arms are held to either side, one grasping part of the robe, the other beckoning with one finger. The hands are shown to be wearing gloves made of delicate interlocking pieces of metal coming to exaggerated fingers ending in points. 

The robe continues up to the neck, encircling the face in a coif and a large hood. The face is covered by a beautiful mask, evoking a porcelain doll, detailed with fine filigree. The coif and mask are seamless, save for two small tentacles reaching out near the right cheek and forehead.[/i][/quote]

Consume supplies, 1 unit of food and water for each character. Tiny characters consume 1/4 rations. Keep track of how much has been consumed.

As evening falls on the first day the players notice a mostly eaten corpse in the road. It appears to be from a human who was wearing now tattered leather armor. A broken crossbow, 7 bolts, and a ruined waterskin can be found next to the body. A DC 13 medicine check can reveal that most of the damage was from claws. A success of +5 or greater reveals the majority of the claws marks were from behind.

At night the players will be ambushed by two moorbounders.

The weather of the next day is warm, windless, and heavy rain. The rain is so heavy vision is lightly obscured (disadvantage Perception checks).

There is no risk of losing their way, as long as the characters travel during the day. If they travel at night the DC for getting lost is 5 (survival). A fail of this check sends them 4 miles to the wrong direction. Travel in heavy rain makes the DC +7.

[b]Zalkin, the Merchant[/b]
When the characters make it to the outpost they meet a mysterious cloaked man with an abishai guard. The man claims to be a merchant and is offering to sell a ring of light. All he asks in return is that the characters one day visit his master's reserve and to tell other's about it. He says there are ruins to the southwest, about a days march. Another 3 days to the southwest is his master's reserve.

[b]Entering the mine[/b]

[i]The staircase circles around 4 times before ending in a small, pitch black room. In the light it shows walls made of heavy cobblestones, a cobble floor, and a wooden door to the south. 

[/i]The door is unlocked.

[i]Beyond the door is a short hall that opens up into a large room. Cabinets and a sink are along the northern wall. The southern wall has a piano and a chest. The center of the room has a table with plates set up, ready for eating. One plate has very old rotten food on it. To the east are two doors, one ajar. Within the rooms are beds, end tables, and small chests. Another door is in the southwest corner of the room.[/i]

Both chests are unlocked and empty.

[i]The door opens into a hallway which turns eastward, opening up into a smaller room. At the far end of the room is a large metal device, looking somewhat like a forge. Thick pipes exit the ground and attach on the left of the machine and connect the right to the wall. The left pipe has a lever on it, in the down position. A skeleton's hand is gripped on the lever and lays crumpled on the ground in front. The front of the device is made of an incredibly thick glass window as well as a smaller window on the top. The front window has a small crack in the glass. Within a coiling moving shape shape made of pure flame can be seen. The north side of the room has a large hole in the wall with rubble surrounding it. The south has a collapsed portion of wall, seemingly burying a minecart and mine tracks.[/i]

Any person entering starting their turn within 25 feet of the furnace takes 1d4 fire damage per round. To lever is partially welded shut from consistent heat exposure. To push it up in the off position, a DC 18 athletics check must be made.

Moving the rubble on the minecart takes 1d6+2 hours of work, -1 for each person assisting, and half as much with a shovel.

Holes in kruthik cavern all over the walls that they swarm out of.

On the way back, the players take a slightly different path and encounter a massive chasm. Walking along it for a time they happen upon a section of chasm 2 miles wide. Floating above the chasm about 500 feet above ground level is a massive mote of earth. Just above the edge a tall thin tower can be seen.
