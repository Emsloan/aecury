When a mind flayer is separated from an elder brain and is unable to feed, it can rarely learn to subsist on pure psionic or magical energy. Achieving this state allows it a respite from base desires, freeing its immense intellect to pursue a grander purpose. These flayers will continue fasting and spend most of their time in a meditative, almost deathlike state. After a long time, sometimes months or even years, it can attain enlightenment, unlocking the great psionic ability of transference and evolving into a mind keeper.

The newly awakened intellect will then seek out settlements or large gatherings of sentient creatures. Mind keepers will secretly hide among them, often posing as beggars or avoiding detection altogether by inhabiting sewers or abandoned buildings. Once settled they begin to feed on the surface thoughts of the citizens all around them, like one might sample the offerings on a large menu. 

Eventually, a mind keeper will single out a specific creature that is close to death. Often its target is sickly or aged, though sometimes it will choose creatures who have suffered near fatal accidents. The mind keeper stalks its target relentlessly, waiting for the inevitable moment of its demise. When the moment finally approaches the mind keeper will reveal itself to the creature, using psionic powers to calm the creature's fears and alleviate its pain. As soon as the creature dies the mind keeper works quickly, initiating the process of transference. 

The mind keeper's body will begin to transform, violently morphing until it resembles an indistinguishable copy of its recently deceased target. Then, it ejects itself from its old body, in the form of a bloated tentacled centipede. Climbing into the mouth of the dead creature, it burrows through the soft palate and into the cranium via the foramen magnum. It proceeds to merge with the brain, rapidly changing its structure not unlike a rapid form of pupation.

After approximately six seconds the transference is complete and the mind keeper now takes over the body of the creature. The process of transference alters the new body, spontaneously healing wounds and curing most ailments. While it largely resembles its old form, over time the mind keeper will develop the telltale signs of its illithidae heritage, and after a few weeks it could not pass as the original creature, let alone a member of its body's original species.

The mind keeper then stages its old body, now a perfect facsimile of the recently deceased creature, so that nothing is amiss when it is finally discovered. It will reproduce wounds, clothe, and pose it to ensure the transference is secret. 

While the physical act of transference is quick, the act of transferring a mind is decadently slow. A mind keeper will 'digest' the mind, consuming each memory, fact, and personality quirk over the course of many years. A mind keeper can call upon this host of experiences, granting it the skills and knowledge of the many minds it has preserved. 

The greatest difference between mind keepers and their flayer siblings is their outlook and personality. While flayers are arrogant, tyrannical, supremacists; keepers are stoic, ascetic, and introspective. They view themselves and other creatures as part of a universal shared consciousness. Keepers consider mind flayer attempts at world domination an embarrassing goal, motivated by a crucial misunderstanding of this belief. 

This perspective can lead keepers to develop nihilistic tendencies, as they consider themselves and all creatures an infinitesimal part of a greater whole. Keepers still have alien minds, but not as foreign as common illithids. Keepers are interested in humanoid lives and cultures and are often fascinated by the minutiae that define them. 

Young keepers often have trouble resisting the influence of a newly collected mind, and will often find their personality temporarily altered after a transference. Older keepers learn to ignore these insistent voices but often develop subtle ticks.

Mind keepers still possess psionic abilities, but lack the brutal mental and physical strength a fresh supply of sentient brains provides. Instead, they develop fine control over their mental powers and learn to mastery of their bodies. Keepers are adept at reading minds without detection, have nearly perfect memories, can telekinetically manipulate multiple objects simultaneously, and can enhance or suppress specific emotions in others. 

Mind keepers are exceedingly hard to kill. If their bodies are damaged beyond their ability to heal, they can simply take over a recently deceased corpse. As long as the brain is not completely destroyed, they can eject themselves and attempt to escape, though they cannot survive for long outside of a host. 

This Mind keepers are known to haunt battlefields, hospitals, or the slums of large cities.
