In the times before Ao, Aecury was home to the elves. The elves believed that they were the progeny of Inferniax. 

A god of flame, life, and light; it was said that Inferniax loathed darkness and the unknown. Thus he willed his light to illuminate every shadowed recess of creation. 

To this end Inferniax imbued the formless gray clay of Aecury with a spark of his violet flame. 
The creations, naught but ichor, displeased Inferniax. 

So he molded the clay into suitable vessels, and so they were; finely wrought things of flesh and bone. Though satisfied with the forms, Inferniax saw they were inanimate. Possessing no will of their own, they merely rested motionless like lamps unlit, lest he spurred them forward through his own desire.

When it came to matters of will, Inferniax knew who of his ilk should be consulted. And so Inferniax entreated Mimid the Wanderer to force his creations to have wills of their own. 

Mimid was mocking in their response, as was their way. This enraged Inferniax, causing his violet flames to sear bright. Mimid did as his nature demanded, responding in kind with golden flames of even greater brilliance. 

The two raged in this way for 9 days; each trying to outshine the other. On the 9th day, their combined white radiance reached into the deepest Void, even causing the slumbering fell beasts who lived there to stir. However, his strength finally faltered. Inferniax's flame diminished til only the faintest ember remained. 

Mimid, momentarily blinded and enveloped by sudden darkness, was alone in creation for the very first time. No wills cried out save one: Their own. The cacophony of desires finally silenced, Mimid reflected in the quickly waning moment of solace. Then, Mimid made the first decision of their existence.

Perhaps rebelling against their ordained purpose, or maybe out of empathy for Inferniax's mindless puppets, Mimid sliced their palm and let the hot blood flow freely onto the clay creations. They gorged themselves on Mimid's essence, itself an amalgam of pure desire. The blood suffused their bodies and in doing so became their own blood, their own essence. As Mimid's vision returned, thine own will nearly submerged in the cacophony once more, he made a pact with the nascent elves. Regardless of time or circumstance, they could call Mimid's domain their own.

After some time, Inferniax regained his strength, finding no sign of Mimid. However, he saw that his creations were thriving and this pleased him greatly. Already they were learning, building, and most importantly spreading across the whole of Aecury. Within each newly born elf was a new violet spark. When each elf died, the spark held within rejoined Inferniax, allowing his light to reach even further. In this way, Inferniax grew his influence in a way he could not on his own, and slowly he grew vast and mighty. For a good many eons he contentedly bathed all of Aecury in his violet warmth.
