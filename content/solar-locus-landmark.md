The Solar Locus contains a single expansive room with 50' arched ceilings. The room has no windows or openings, save for a sizeable cutaway in the northern ceiling. Sticking out into the air is a massive glass disc, covered in vines, and like a bunch of glass grapes on brass stems, dozens of progressively smaller lenses arc downward toward the southern side of the room. There, a series of seven, concentric recessed platforms descend, creating a 15' diameter space at the bottom. In the center a small hole, with a distinct shape, is cut into the stone floor. Upon closer inspection faint grooves in the shape of curved rectangles can be seen radiating out from the hole. On the northern side a few feet from the hole in the floor, an empty basin resides. Beyond it a dark north facing tunnel runs under the floor.

## Purpose

The Solar Locus is a forge, foundry, and smithy. Using a series of magically imbued lenses it focuses sunlight into a beam of light capable of melting and heating nearly any material. Through a complex system of levers and pullies, the lenses can be configured to provide perfect heating for any type of metalworking.
