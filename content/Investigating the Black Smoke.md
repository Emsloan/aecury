A plume of black smoke can sometimes be seen far in the distance, south of [[Raft]].

A message from [[Lightfoot]] has gathered a group of adventurers to go and investigate. [[Otiz]], [[Sussarra Kurva]], and [[Vulpes]] have shown up.

[b]The outpost is 30 miles southwest of Raft, as the crow flies.[/b]

The weather of the first day of travel is warm, normal for spring, approximately 80 degrees. There is no breeze and the sky is cloudy. A DC 12 Nature check indicates that rain is likely in the next few days.

There is no risk of losing their way, as long as the characters travel during the day. If they travel at night the DC for getting lost is 5 (survival). A fail of this check sends them 4 miles to the wrong direction

At midday, the players notice a large statue on their path. Examining the statue shows it is toppled. Read the following description:

[quote]
[i]It appears to depict a heavily robed female figure in intricately worked marble. The statue is broken around the waist and the top half lays to the right side.

Where the bottom of the robe would meet the ground, large octopus-like tentacles spill out in all directions in place of feet. The top half's arms are held to either side, one grasping part of the robe, the other beckoning with one finger. The hands are shown to be wearing gloves made of delicate interlocking pieces of metal coming to exaggerated fingers ending in points. 

The robe continues up to the neck, encircling the face in a coif and a large hood. The face is covered by a beautiful mask, evoking a porcelain doll, detailed with fine filigree. The coif and mask are seamless, save for two small tentacles reaching out near the right cheek and forehead.[/i][/quote]


Consume supplies, 1 unit of food and water for each character. Tiny characters consume 1/4 rations. Keep track of how much has been consumed.

[b]Daytime approach:[/b]
As the players approach the smoke, from about 1000 feet out they first see a large chasm snaking across the land. The smoke seems to be emanating from within. Nearby is a simple stone structure. East of the chasm is a cave.

If daytime, ask for a DC 13 Perception check from everyone in the party. Anyone who succeeds notices a handful of figures creeping towards the structure from the north. Beating the DC by more than 5 reveals they are humanoid and have weapons drawn.

[b]Nighttime approach:[/b]
As the players approach the smoke, from about 1000 feet out they first see a large chasm snaking across the land. Nearby is a simple stone structure, with flickering fire light emanating from within. East of the chasm is a mound of earth (actually a cave).

Ask for a DC 16 perception check to discern the source of the smoke. A success correctly indicates the smoke is coming from within the chasm, a failure appears as if its coming from the structure. A success of 5 or more reveals that another, very small light source can be seen elsewhere, around 500 feet north of the building.

[b]Entering the mine[/b]

[i]The staircase circles around 4 times before ending in a small, pitch black room. In the light it shows walls made of heavy cobblestones, a cobble floor, and a wooden door to the south. 

[/i]The door is locked, DC 12 with thieves tools.

[i]Beyond the door is a short hall that opens up into a large room. Cabinets and a sink are along the northern wall. The southern wall has a piano and a chest. The center of the room has a table with plates set up, ready for eating. One plate has very old rotten food on it. To the east are two doors, one ajar. Within the rooms are beds, end tables, and small chests. Another door is in the southwest corner of the room.[/i]

Both chests are locked, DC 8. The north one contains 16 cp and an antitoxin, the south contains 9gp and very old sheaf of paper, with the journal entry.

[i]The door opens into a hallway which turns eastward, opening up into a smaller room. At the far end of the room is a large metal device, looking somewhat like a forge. Thick pipes exit the ground and attach on the left of the machine and connect the right to the wall. The left pipe has a lever on it, in the down position. A skeleton's hand is gripped on the lever and lays crumpled on the ground in front. The front of the device is made of an incredibly thick glass window as well as a smaller window on the top. Within a coiling moving shape shape made of pure flame can be seen. The north side of the room has a large hole in the wall with rubble surrounding it. The south has a collapsed portion of wall, seemingly burying a minecart and mine tracks.[/i]

Any person entering the range or starting their turn within 25 feet of the furnace takes 1d4 fire damage per round. To lever is partially welded shut from consistent heat exposure. To push it up in the off position, a DC 18 athletics check must be made.

Moving the rubble on the minecart takes 1d6+2 hours of work, -1 for each person assisting, and half as much with a shovel.
