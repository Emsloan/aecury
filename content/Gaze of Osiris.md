Every thirteen years, the night sky is lit by the eclipse of [[Iadath]] over [[Iseolus]] . During this time the [[Abydons]] partake in nocturnal festivities. The morning of the eclipse the [[Abydons]] , dress themselves in their most beloved garments and begin to forage for [[Eyes of Osiris]] . This tradition is upheld to remind the people of their voyage in to these lands. The destruction of their favorite garments is meant to represent the life and death cycle, they must by the end of the eclipse, have fully destroyed their garments. This brings about a time new life, where [[Abydons]] spend the next few months creating new and lavish clothings.

When the eclipse begins, the people consume their [[Eyes of Osiris]] and begin to gather around the main temple. Great feasts are prepared and bonfires sprout all around the temple. Rythmic music, mostly made by drums, various reed flutes, and other wind instruments, persist throughout the night. [[Abydons]] dance and sing along, creating amongst themselves small dense groups of bodies that mingle throughout other groups. Competitions of strength, speed, dance and wit also pervade this festival, and the winners of these competitions hold bragging rights until the next [[Gaze of Osiris]] . 

The most desirable competition to win, is the [[Vitalitas]] . The winner of this dance earns the honorific title of Vitalitas. This represents a conquering of death, and this individual is seen to be full of life and youth.
