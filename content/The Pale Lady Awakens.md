The Wolf has found its game
The Stag has lost the trail
The Bear forgets its name
And the Owl cries tears of hail

The leaf is thin and ruddy
The grass has fled the hills
The sky is gray and clowdy
But the moon now spies its thrills

The children are numb and disordered
The elder is placid no more
The fauna is famished and hunting
The chains are securing the door

The torches are lit and angry
The crooks are sharpened and fierce
The night has come
Misted wings stalk the stars

A frigid embrace awaits

Inspired by the Lament of the Wind, “The air screams and the winds cry. For the Pale Lady, draped in Alabaster Mists, has awoken.”
