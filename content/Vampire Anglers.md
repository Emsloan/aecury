Indistinguishable from branches when stationary, these large insects hide in the canopy and wait patiently for creatures to walk underneath. When one comes within range, they launch their stingers, impaling the unsuspecting prey. The stinger remains connected to the angler by a strong sinewy cord. The stinger is promptly reeled in, yanking the prey off the ground where it can only ineffectually flail. Then the vampire angler begins to feed, draining the blood of its catch. Once the blood is emptied the angler retracts the barbs on its stinger, dropping the corpse to the ground. Some say these desiccated corpses can warn of the vampire anglers presence, but by the time travelers notice them its usually too late.

Stealth +5
False Appearance (Object Form Only). While the bug remains motionless, it is indistinguishable from an ordinary branch.

Actions:

[b]Barbed Stinger. [/b]Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 20/60 ft., one target. Hit: 11 (2d6 + 4) piercing damage. On a hit, the target is grappled (escape DC 12). Escaping from the grapple causes the target to take 6 piercing damage as the barbed stinger is ripped from its body. While attached, the bug doesn't attack. At the start of each of the bug's turns, the target loses 5 (1d4 + 3) hit points due to blood loss.

Cord Hit points

[b]Retract Barbs. [/b]As a reaction to taking damage, the vampire angler can retract the barb in its stinger and releasing any currently impaled prey.

Bonus actions:

[b]Pull. [/b]If the target is a Large or smaller creature, the vampire angler may retract its stinger that is currently impaling a target. The target must succeed on a Strength contest against the bug or be pulled up to 20 feet toward it.
