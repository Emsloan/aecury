There once was a merchant name Kel who was known across the lands to be fair and true in all his dealings. However, he lived among a wretched people who held no regard for honesty. His competitors would use false weights and rigged scales to take more than their due and they grew fat and wealthy from the excess. Over time, the merchant became thin and impoverished. There were those who would ask, "Why does he not lie and cheat? All other merchants do what they must to get ahead."

Eventually word reached the [[Pontiff Ascendi]]‌ and [[Vox Platini Dei]]‌ that the merchant's people were corrupt and known to consort with demons. The Pontiff, sworn to stamp out all darkness, immediately declared an extermination. The Vox, dedicated to justice, insisted on temperance. And so it was agreed, if one truly honest person could be found among the pitiful wretches, they would be granted decimation.

The forces of Platinum and Gold swept accross the evil lands and seized control. They round up each merchant, for among them the most honest should be found, and tested each of their scales and weights. Each merchant was found to be deceitful and was burnt in a cleansing vessel. After 9 days it seemed all merchants were tested and all were found lacking. The Pontiff insisted the extermination begin, and the Vox insisted on staying his hand. "Grant me one more day, and I will bring you an honest man."

And so the Vox went among the people and asked, "Is there not one among you whose weights are fair and scales are balanced?" All said no, but the Vox was touched by the Platinum Lord, and so he heard the truth in the hearts of men. The people were lying, protecting Kel in return for his unwavering honor, unaware of the extermination at hand. The Vox found Kel and tested his scales and weights; they were perfectly balanced and true.

So it was that the decimation began. Lots were drawn and 1 in 10 were burned in a cleansing vessel. On the last day of the decimation, when the final 10 lots were drawn, it was Kel who was to be cleansed. The Vox was outraged, for surely it was unjust to kill one so honest. The Pontiff knew it was the will of the [[Amaunator]]‌, and insisted the cleansing proceed. The Vox and Pontiff argued and raged, each fighting for what they believed was right.

Their squabble was interrupted when Kel stepped into the cleansing vessel of his own accord and lit himself aflame. He did not scream or thrash, instead standing tall with scales in hand.

As the flames sputtered Kel's body was naught but char in the shape of a man. As the flames died a terrible gust began to blow, stripping the ash from his bones. As the gust subsided the sky parted and pure light shon upon him. Where once was flesh, was now only bones, skeleton and scales wrought from crystal and gleaming. And so Kelemvor came to be.
