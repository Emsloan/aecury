## The Birth of Aecury

When the world of Aecury was young in the cosmos great forces rose from its core so that life and meaning could be brought to its surface. Those forces were known as the Primordials and the Intrinsics. The Primordials of Aecury were the unwavering building blocks of the planet, the world as it was. The Intrinsics of Aecury were the mercurial philosophies of the planet, the world as it saw itself. Together these ancient and powerful beings formed all that would come to exist in the world of Aecury.

The eldest and most powerful of the Primordials, Inferniax the Violet, rose from the core of Aecury and found the surface dark and cold. Inferniax settled amidst the void and filled the sky with his light.. With his fire shining brightly on Aecury, Inferniax took the title Lord of Flame. Next, Tannatar the Grey rose from the core of Aecury and found its surface uniform and plain. Tannatar thrust her heel into the ground and it erupted forming mountains and canyons alike. With her structures giving variance to the surface, Tannatar took the title Groundbreaker. Third, Cyran the Teal rose from the core of Aecury and found its surface harsh and feverish. Cyran opened their arms and water flowed up from the deepest canyons until half of the world was ocean. With their waters cooling Aecury, Cyran took the title Master of Depths. Finally the youngest and most frantic of the Primordials, Waernah the Pale, rose from the core of Aecury and found its surface stagnant and passive. Waernah opened their mouth and their breath filled the skies of Aecury. With their winds churning the waters and shearing the mountains, Waernah took the title Skyborne.

When the Primordials had shaped the world as they saw fit, the Intrinsics rose to look upon their creations. The eldest and most powerful of the Intrinsics, Maliga, rose from the core of Aecury and chastised the Primordials for creating only in their image. With spite in her heart Maliga created grass and trees, as well as kelp and coral, so that the Primordials could see how they had limited themselves. With her lesson given, Maliga took the title Spiteful. Next Phobos rose to look upon the world and found it exposed and vulnerable. With fear in their heart Phobos created shade and the night, so that the Primordials could see how careless they had been. With their lesson given, Phobos took the title Fearful.

Yada yada yada.

Finally the youngest and most fleeting of the Intrinsics, Mimid, rose from the core to look upon the creations of their siblings and the Primordials. Much to the surprise of all the greater powers Mimid saw the world they had made and wept. The creations of the Primordials and Intrinsics were beautiful, and they knew this, but they lacked the spark that the Primordials and Intrinsics had. With a heavy heart Mimid opened their wrists and began to travel the world, granting the creatures of Aecury the gift of their blood and Will, so that they too might make Aecury what they wanted. With their sacrifice given, Mimid took the title Willful.

## The Murals

The walls on either side of the stairs are full of murals, if asked who made them:

"I'm not sure, it may have been Uursovk, the most scholarly of the court members. They often were the one telling me stories of the old world and how they had made some discovery that would change the universe. Then Maladar would step in and tell me that the past is behind us, the only thing we can do now is look for a future that we create ourselves… He wasn't always an ass. I did love looking at these drawings though, the way they depicted the people, they're quite lovely."

The first stretch of murals show Long eared humanoids always looking up at thousands of stars and constellations, pointing and sketching, wandering vast valleys and fields following maps in the sky. After a couple dozen feet they switch to small villages and collections of people gathering at the bottom of mountains, only one or two individuals are ever seen looking down to the rest from raised ground, everyone else is always looking up. Then the murals go to large cities of thousands and thousands, all dressed in fine clothes, there's a consistent and ever-present flow going through the air that the people are breathing. Again, only one or two individuals are ever seen looking down from raised plinths and daises, though now there are 7 others close beneath them looking straight ahead.

After a 5 minute walk still moving down with more murals moving through the cities and seeing strange structures and machines dotting the streets and skies, the murals abruptly change. Now the bottom of the murals are always clouds, as though the people in them have ascended from the earth and show them dwelling amongst mountain peaks. Large arches are seen in various spots with a strange spiral in the center of them. Each one has a small circular hole at the base. After a bit the murals pan down and zoom out to keep the peaks in frame but also show millions of people gathered in the mountain ranges below those who walk among the peaks.

There is only one figure now whose features you can make out amongst a sea of smaller forms who all have their hands raised and heads bent back towards the sky. This figure is depicted being about as tall as Clavis or the rider on the wall, but when scaled to the people at their feet, they stand above the peaks and clouds. It's a more feminine form though no gender is discernible, wearing a flowing robe with millions of intricacies and patterns.

The murals from here on are much sloppier, some seeming unfinished, others all together scratched out, some have words in a language you can't understand painted on top:

"If I had to guess I suppose it would probably be the Queen who did that."
"she isn't actually the Queen, I've just never heard her or anyone else refer to her as otherwise, so it has become her name I guess."

I suppose the queen may have been just one, and Maladar loved to present himself as royalty. Most disagreements within the court were between Maladar and Rall, or Lord Rall is what they referred to themselves as, they and Uursovk were the ones I spent my free time with. Those two had helped instill in me a love for this place, told me to appreciate it as my home. Malgas was a killjoy but they too had a passion for this place and its denizens. The rest however, well let's just say they were want to relive the glory days.

"If I had to count the number of times they claimed we should find some sort of hole in the defenses of those who locked them here, well I don't think I could to be frank… Do you know what tentacle-faces plan is? Aside from forcing our hands to murder of course."

The murals that aren't defaced show a scattered people, and an ambiguous form like a hand coming from the stars and picking up the very mountains that the long-eared people had congregated in, and the titan-like form of the One holding the sphere and the ring is nowhere to be found. The murals then are scattered, with stretches of 30-40 feet between them, each showing different sights that are almost familiar to you all at this point, open grassless plains with sparse trees, some with leaves falling upward. A massive canyon with a giant mountain range, almost like a bowl containing whatever was unlucky enough to be put inside. And strange creatures of a multitude of sizes and species, some recognizable and others utter nightmares, falling to the ground from above the sea of endless clouds in the sky.

The final mural you see, before the stairs turn into a hallway, shows a stretch of land leading up to a massive tower. Dozens of spiraling holes are shown at various parts of the sky and ground. Next there appears to be three Long eared individuals standing at the center of all the spirals, though something is off about their appearance. They point to the strange holes with one hand and have the other on the hilts of blades hanging on their wastes. Next there are armies of long-eared figures, each in a similar stance from the three in the prior section, marching in unison through the holes, into darkness.

And the murals end.
