It is said that the Church of Righteous Death is the oldest body of power in the [[Historian Syncretate]] existing before the time of [[Weeping Mountains]]. Little is known about the high-priests of [[Kelemvor]], and even less is known of their practices within the castle walls. The only time the high-priests are visible to the public is during a grand trial, positioned to either side of the Iudex Primeri and at the subsequent executions of any criminals who are deemed Noxium. It is believed that those who serve Kelemvor are his eyes and hands on the prime material, guiding those who leave the earth to the Crystal Scale. Some of the oldest denizens of Historia tell of fairy tales from the days before the skies fell, of times when the great army of Historia continued to serve its people even after death. Those who know these tales even claim that those armies still exist beneath the city, waiting to be called to action once more.

## Origins

The Apotheosis of Kel implies that worship of Kelemvor began sometime after the offices of the Vox Platini Dei and Pontiff Ascendi were established. Records on the war with Kelaemvalk from the 24th century hold many references to slaves who already practiced Kelemvor woship. It is believed that the crusade referenced in the Apotheosis of Kel is the in fact the same as the conflict with Kelaemvalk. The peoples of Kelaemvalk worshipped a good many pagan gods, and naturally at least one was a god of death. Following the miraculous apotheosis of Kel and the pressures of the crusade to join the Historian fold, it seems natural to assume that many of those pagans came to be devout followers of Kelemvor.
