## Seeded

Live in canyons, less than 1000 population, live in the worm carved tunnels. Lead to an oasis by what they believe to be a purple worm. Was actually a neolithid. That neolithid still lives, acting as their secret leader. It was originally dull, barely possessing sentience. The sandswimmers offered sacrifice as thanks and worshipped it as a god. The sacrifices slowly increased its intelligence. Eventually, ritualistically, the old and infirm Sandswimmers would go to the neolithid, offering themselves. Their experiences and personalities affected the neolithid, slowly turning it good.

## History

In a long gone age there was a people who lived in a paradise. One day, the people of this paradise were approached by a great spirit, known as the Coiled One. The Coiled One warned of a coming [[calamity]] and offered to lead them to a place that would be spared. The Coiled One was a bizarre creature and the paradise's sages claimed it was born of evil. Many ignored the Coiled One's warning, believing it a trick. Those who didn't followed the Coiled One and, true to its word, were taken to an oasis beneath the sand. The calamity came shortly after but the faithful Sandswimmers were spared.

The Sandswimmers brought nothing from paradise, save for a single blade. Prophecy states that one day a worthy hero will appear, claiming the blade, and leading the way back to their ancestral paradise.

## Culture

The people of the Sandswimmers believe that one shouldn't judge based solely on appearances or reputation. One should be judged by their actions. They value shrewdness, healthy suspicion, and experimentation. These qualities make them excellent traders, willing to take risks on strange merchants with stranger goods.

The Sandswimmers revere the Coiled One and by extension many bizarre aberrations. They live alongside a Flumph cloister and have twin Spectators who guarding the inner sanctum of the Coiled One's temple.

The Sandswimmers live nomadic lives. At any point half of the population roams the tunnels created by the purple worms, collection their rare mineral excretions. Purple worms rarely revisit tunnels they've previously created and so the Sandswimmers often lay down roots in these relatively hospitable places.
