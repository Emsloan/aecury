(dark brown-black leather with 6 lines scratched into it) 

There are 6 sections of the book, each separated by a page fully colored in black ink.

[b]First [/b]speaks of their hatred for the "Witch-God" who has forsaken them from their home, and that their arcane gifts are now a curse in this new empty and bleak world. They claim that they will have their vengeance. 

[b]Second [/b]in different handwriting, speaks of the discovery of one just like them who they believe could be a successor in their plans, then suddenly transitions into a discussion of how to extend the consciousness of one “who controls the weave" outside of forcing their soul into this container. It goes on to make plans for its uprising against its former Elder. 

[b]Third [/b]speaks of the voices, saying that they are getting louder with each additional mind and muses that someday the voices will be all that remains. It writes that its breakthrough for the transference of a humanoid life force thereby extending their own has fallen flat in the faces of these old fools.

[b]Fourth [/b]speaks of this 'curse of succession, and that this existence has been nothing but pain and wasted effort since the completion of the former's goal. "After the 'Great Freeing' and elimination of The Brains the long eared rulers of this world have grown tired of our antics, and since the gate was damaged and the God in Yellow freed some of the others, all of the denizens are on edge. I feel a war brewing, with the celestials bolstering their numbers... I want nothing more than to be free of this place." 

[b]Fifth[/b] section page has the name Zagrudull on it; it speaks of a fierce hatred in its heart that sometimes allows it to block out the voices, and that it intends to use its power and hatred to achieve its goals, ending with 'These warring insects will bow before me. I will make this realm feel the cold embrace of my will. 

[b]Sixth [/b]has the name Lusgidall, and chronicles Lusgidall's improvements of the periapt and how they have made it more of a tool than a relic. It theorizes that humanoids might not be the only life forces that can be added to their lifespan, and then discusses the idea of them taking monsters as well as humanoids. Though the conversion is lower for monsters, they are more abundant in certain cases, most certainly in this wasteland. One paragraph speaks of their need to find an heir, and that if the Onyx Gaze is to conquer all, as their predecessor intended, the heir will need to truly be a terror that the world beyond has never known. Towards the end of the section the writing becomes shakier and more intense, “The imbeciles who I once could order on a whim have grown bold in these times. Only my fellow mages and scholars share my vision. I will make those traitors pay." The last few pages shift
greatly in tone, from desperate and pleading to inquisitive and aloof. One paragraph reads "Have we been blinded by our
thirst for freedom? Do the powers of this waste and its god hold secrets that I have not considered?" And in another
passage, "My eyes have been opened and my destiny is mine for the taking. I need more test subjects." The final page of
this section has one paragraph with fresh ink on it, “I've done it, the others do not seem convinced, but my experiments with
the beholder and the platinum sphere are all the proof I need. Though the children seemed to be promising, I fear my old
plans pale in comparison to the path that has been revealed to me. Joining our purity with the filth of the rest of existence
was optimistic at best, and an affront to our goals at worst. The key was never in the power of the living. I will no longer wait
for the aid of the Yellow God, I will conquer the Court in the Tower, I will use the creatures in this divine prison as fuel for my
ambition, I will open the door to Aecury and claim it as my kingdom and its people as my slaves. My ascension awaits, all
that is left for me now is everlasting life in death."
